Education

Here I will explain my education in more detail

College

I began studying at Halesowen college in 2020. This affected my studies for the first year because the course was supposed to have many hands on experiment type lessons, but due to Covid we were only able to do one which was with the hardware of a PC (Remove HDD, RAM, fans etc and put it all back together as fast as you can. I actually won this competition). Following the Covid year, 2021 had much less restrictions meaning we got a lot of work done. The topics I had studied were Cyber security, Cloud computing, Mobile technology and Game development. My second year was when I made my VR game for my end of year project.

Attack on Kashyyyk

College end of year project

Attack on Kashyyyk was my original game plan and it was going to be a VR hack and slash game. It was inspired by Gorn, a cartoon VR hack and slash game. I had worked on Attack on Kashyyyk for the entire year, when it came to the final week my file corrupt. With no back-ups I had no choice but to start over, however I only had 3 or 4 days left before the hand in date so instead I made "The museum".

The museum

In this game the player roamed around a museum looking at antiques and other models, before venturing upstairs and pressing the green button to win and the red button to lose... Yeah a very thrilling game. On the brightside, it taught me to always have backups.

University

I began studying at Staffordshire university September 2022. I am currently only 3 months in, but I can see the improvements I have made already. I have made many games, of which will be in the C++ and C# links, and solved multiple problems using code.

C# and C++

Some of my favourite projects I have created since being at university consist of "Marble madness" and "Elf man". Marble madness is a unity remake of the 1980's arcade game. Elf man is a Pacman game, however I made my sprites in correlation to the original "Legend of Zelda". Creating "Elf man" was awfully congusing for me at first because we didn't use a game engine, instead it was on a software called S2D that the university used.

Games Engine Creation

Games Engine Creation is a topic on my university course in which we are given problems and we must solve them using only our programming skills. The first few lessons of this topic were simple, however as the course went on they got more and more complicated.
